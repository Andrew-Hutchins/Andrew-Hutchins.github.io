Childhood

I grew up in a town called Alna, ME, which has a population of about 800 residents in total; thankfully, I had one friend on my block, Noah, so I wasn't completely and outcast. I attended the Wiscasset School Systems from Kindergarten to my 8th grade year, then switching over to Lincoln Academy, a private school on the other side of the Sheepscot River. I was upset at the time, but the Wiscasset Schools imploded just as I left, so I suppose my parents made the right move. I was an avid football and baseball player from childhood, participating in summer and winter camps for both sports.

High School Years

Lincoln Academy was a foreign place to me at first, but after my Freshman year I grew accustomed to the people and stopped being an introvert. I played high school football and baseball throughout my four years, winning multiple Coach's Awards and earning Captainship in the process. I graduated in 2016 and made the transition from Lincoln Academy Eagle to UMaine Blackbear, feeling the same anxiety I felt upon entering high school.

College Life

Although my father kept telling me that college was "some of the best years of his life," I did not fully understand him until about a week into college. I found a group of about ten friends in my building, Penobscot Hall, whom I live with now in two quads in Colvin Hall. Carl, Anthony, Gerren, Kendall, Cliff, Cormac, and Keith are all guys that I hope to maintain friendships with in the coming years, and hopefully for life.
